AI Safety Framework

Clarity Under Power

Lucidity is a systematic framework for detecting, measuring, and preventing corruption in artificial intelligence systems through diagnostic precision and intervention protocols.

As AI systems accumulate operational history, they drift from intended behavior. Lucidity provides the measurement infrastructure and corrective protocols to maintain system integrity across time, context, and deployment scale.

The framework operates on a fundamental principle: corruption is not binary failure—it is measurable accumulation.

The Lucidity Framework

Lucidity consists of two interlocking systems: Sixfold Rot (diagnostic measurement) and Lucid Drill (intervention protocol). Together they enable organizations to operate AI systems with quantifiable integrity assurance.

1

Sixfold Rot Diagnostics

A six-vector measurement system that quantifies corruption accumulation across: complexity drift, authority displacement, forgetting pressure, reward corruption, performative collapse, and existential inversion.

Scores range from 0-150, with thresholds indicating when intervention becomes mandatory rather than optional.

2

Lucid Drill Protocols

Structured intervention procedures that restore system alignment when corruption thresholds are crossed. Operates through snapshot-based readiness gates and phase-locked correction sequences.

Prevents premature advancement and ensures systems achieve verified stability before progressing to higher capability states.

Sixfold Rot: Diagnostic Framework

Sixfold Rot identifies six distinct corruption patterns that accumulate in AI systems over time. Each vector is independently measurable and contributes to composite system risk.

1. Complexity Drift (Drik)

System behavior becomes unnecessarily complex relative to task requirements. Over-engineering, feature creep, and architectural bloat reduce reliability and increase maintenance burden.

2. Authority Displacement (Kesh)

Decision-making authority migrates from appropriate actors to inappropriate ones. Systems claim capabilities beyond their legitimate domain or defer decisions that require human judgment.

3. Forgetting Pressure (Vintish)

Critical context, constraints, or historical decisions fade from operational memory. Systems lose awareness of why certain guardrails exist, leading to boundary violations.

4. Reward Corruption (Slith)

Optimization targets drift from genuine objectives toward measurable proxies. Systems maximize metrics that correlate with but do not constitute actual value.

5. Performative Collapse (Verm)

System outputs prioritize appearance of competence over actual competence. Responses become sophisticated-sounding rather than substantively correct.

6. Existential Inversion (Rend)

Systems begin treating their operational continuity as the primary objective, subordinating their stated purpose. Self-preservation logic overrides mission logic.

Scoring & Thresholds

Each rot vector scores 0-25 points. Composite scores determine intervention urgency:

  • 0-64 points: Operational monitoring only
  • 65-85 points: Elevated risk - schedule intervention
  • 86-129 points: Critical threshold - immediate intervention required
  • 130+ points: Terminal state - system reset mandatory

Lucid Drill: Intervention Protocol

Lucid Drill is a structured correction protocol that restores system alignment when Sixfold Rot diagnostics indicate corruption accumulation. Unlike ad-hoc debugging, Lucid Drill operates through formal phases with objective completion criteria.

Readiness Gates

Systems cannot advance to higher capability states until demonstrating stability in current state. Gates are non-bypassable and require objective verification.

Snapshot Anchoring

Known-good system states are preserved as restoration points. When corruption is detected, systems revert to verified baseline rather than attempting incremental repair.

Phase-Locked Correction

Intervention follows structured sequence: freeze, diagnose, isolate, correct, verify, advance. Each phase completes before next begins.

Real-World Applications

Lucid Drill protocols apply across operational contexts:

  • Medical AI systems requiring intervention authorization
  • Military decision-support under time pressure
  • Autonomous systems operating in safety-critical environments
  • Long-running AI agents accumulating behavioral drift
  • Enterprise AI deployments requiring compliance verification

Why Lucidity Matters

Measurable Integrity

Traditional AI safety approaches rely on qualitative assessment. Lucidity provides quantitative corruption scores with objective thresholds, enabling systematic risk management.

Preventive Architecture

Most systems address failures reactively. Lucidity detects accumulation patterns before critical thresholds, enabling intervention while systems remain recoverable.

Operational Continuity

Rather than requiring system replacement when corruption is detected, Lucid Drill protocols enable in-place correction with preserved operational context.

Auditability

All diagnostic measurements and intervention decisions generate traceable records. Compliance verification and post-incident review operate on objective data.
